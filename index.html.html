👗 Dress Code & Tips

⛪
Churches

Knees and shoulders must be covered. Carry a light scarf at all times — entry will be refused otherwise.

👟
Footwear

Flip flops for the beach only — not appropriate for towns, restaurants, or churches.

🛍️
Shop Etiquette

Always greet on entry (Buongiorno / Buonasera). Say goodbye when leaving (Arrivederci). Do not touch merchandise without asking. Browsing without greeting is considered rude.

💶
Tipping

Not expected like in the US. A coperto on your bill is normal — covers bread and table service. For a large group, leaving 10–20€ cash for your server is generous. Tips cannot be added to credit card — cash only.

Free Time in Burgio
"City of Bells and Ceramics" · Arab-Norman mountain village
▶

Founded around 1000 AD, Burgio sits on the slopes of the Sicani Mountains. Its name derives from the Arabic "burǧ" (tower/fortress). Norman Count Roger I captured it in 1087. Today it's known as Sicily's "City of Bells and Ceramics."

🚶
"Town of 100 Portals" — Historic Center

Winding Arab-medieval alleys with 100+ intricately carved stone portals and ceramic-decorated balconies. The Rabato quarter wraps tightly around the castle.

⛪
Chiesa di San Giuseppe

The "Sistine Chapel of the Sicani Mountains" — 17th-century church with a majolica-tiled bell tower and fully frescoed Baroque interior. May be closed in the afternoon.

🏰
Castello di Burgio

Walk up to the ruins for panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. (Driver likely cannot take you to the historic center — you'll need to walk.)

☕
Local Coffee Bar

If you find an open bar, enter and ask about finding Piazza relatives — "old-timers" know the family names of the area. Most won't speak English, but it can be very interesting!

⚠️ Note: You'll arrive mid-afternoon — the town may appear quiet and deserted with very little open. The best thing to do is take a walk through the streets. The castle requires walking on your own.
